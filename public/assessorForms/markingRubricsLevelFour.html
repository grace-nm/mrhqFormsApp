LEVEL 4 MARKING RUBRICS

Component Competency Statement Evidence you will provide
1. Analyse and select best fit solutions of existing and upcoming technologies in order to develop a technology product solution.
Analysis and selection of technical assets to accelerate product development. • Rationale for selecting technical assets applied in product development is explained, including: o the benefits and limitations of technical assets o Choice of any assets selected is explained • The assets selected are appropriate for the product solution. Technical assets may include, but are not limited to; development languages, code libraries, and frameworks. Appropriate selection means that assets are selected because they will enhance the efficacy or efficiency of development, or the usability, performance, functionality, security, or reliability of the product solution. Project evaluation form
Analysis of upcoming technologies which may be applied to the product solution • At least one upcoming technology is identified, and a web link is provided as a reference • An upcoming technology is analysed as it would apply to the product solution, including discussions of benefits, strengths, and/or limitations to the product solution. (Upcoming technologies may include, but are not limited to: artificial intelligence and virtual reality). Project evaluation form
2. Apply broad technical knowledge to develop a technology product solution.
Complete requirements for the Mission Functionality completed meets at least: • Half of the Technical Design requirements of the screen, AND • Half of the Functional Requirements of the screen, AND • Works in at least one of the four major browsers (Safari, Chrome, FireFox, or Edge) or Mobile device Product demonstration Project evaluation form Source control / GitHub
Professional organisation of code At least four (4) of the following are demonstrated: • Variables declared at the beginning of their appropriate scope • related functions are grouped together in the same file • minimum redundant/non-used code • technical assets organised in logical folder structure • in-line comment to state naming conventions • files, variables, and functions are named with appropriate names and follows a consistent convention (either snake case or camel case) • relevant, inline comments to explain less intuitive logic • Separation of business logic from presentation layer • Separation of business logic from data storage Product demonstration Source control / GitHub
3. Apply self-directed learning and self-reflection techniques with the purpose of becoming a lifelong learner.
Apply self-directed research to the product solution • Research is identified and described with appropriate references provided • How self-directed research was applied to the product solution is described • Code snippets or other technical assets from self-directed research, are appropriately applied to the product solution. Examples of appropriate application include: code, images or assets are used to enhance the usability, performance, functionality, security, or reliability of the product solution. Product demonstration Project evaluation form
Reflect on own learning and professional development In project evaluation form, the learner has: • reflected on their experience of developing a technology solution • identified areas of strength within the project or their own performance • Identified areas for their own professional development, including at least one (1) technical or coding area Self reflection form
4. Apply security and technology product development best practices to deliver quality outcome in the context of practice.
Apply cybersecurity considerations Security measures which were applied or could be applied are present as: • Relevant inline comments in code, OR • Explained separately on project evaluation form. Project evaluation form Source control / GitHub
Ensure code quality Demonstrate at least one (1) of the following in source control tool: • testing and defect fixing (e.g. writing comments to highlight bug fixes) • evidence of using tools to ensure code quality (e.g. static analysis tool) • evidence of formative peer review of code (e.g. change suggestion comments within code, or evidence of a formal code review in GitHub) Product demonstration Source control / GitHub
Collaborate using appropriate tooling • Collaboration are visible in the source control • Collaboration of team members is visible in the source control as interaction or code push throughout the Mission. Source control / GitHub
5. Apply collaboration skills to work as an effective team member.
Organise time to meet deadlines Attended meetings and delivered work on time, as per evaluation by the facilitator and at least half of peers in the team Peer review Assessor evaluation
Demonstrate mutual respect Demonstrated mutual respect, per evaluation by the facilitator and at least half of peers in the team Peer review Assessor evaluation
Communicate ideas effectively Ideas communicated effectively, per evaluation by the facilitator and at least half of peers in the team Peer review Assessor evaluation
Listen to the point of views of others Listen to the point of views of others Listened to the point of view of others, per evaluation by the facilitator and at least half of peers in the team. Peer review Assessor evaluation
Allocate roles and work fairly Taken on a fair share of end-to-end product development responsibility in the team (e.g. in a team of 3 full stack developers, responsible for developing roughly 1/3 of the product features), per described in project evaluation The student assessed is responsible for at least two (2) pages in the finished application, including at least one (1) page that has both front end and back end functionality Peer review Assessor evaluation Source control / Github
